## Its AI-Powered Health Information Retrieval Agent

**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

March 17, 2026

Contact: ssasikumar@collabiora.com | collabiora.com

LOS ANGELES, CA — collabiora, an AI-powered health literacy platform bridging patients, clinical researchers, and the healthcare industry, today announced the filing of a provisional patent application with the United States Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO) for Yori, its proprietary AI agent and health information retrieval system. The filing marks a pivotal milestone as the Los Angeles-based healthtech startup moves to protect its core artificial intelligence technology and expand its footprint in the rapidly growing medical AI market.

---

## Yori: “Helpful” by design

Yori — whose name means “helpful” in Japanese — is a large language model (LLM)-based AI agent purpose-built for medical research intelligence. Unlike general-purpose AI chatbots, Yori operates exclusively within curated, peer-reviewed biomedical sources.

Its proprietary multi-pipeline architecture combines natural language query processing, clinical trial matching, AI synthesis of research findings, and expert identification — capabilities that collectively replace what previously required three to four separate clinical research tools.

> “We kept meeting patients who had no idea a relevant clinical trial existed for them, and researchers who couldn’t cut through the noise fast enough to help. Yori was built to close that gap. Filing this patent is our commitment to making sure it stays that way.” — Sanskriti Sasikumar, MD, PhD, Co-Founder & CEO, collabiora

---

## Built for retrieval, synthesis, and personalization

At a technical level, Yori’s patented architecture features:

- A hybrid LLM-assisted intent classification system
- Multi-source parallel retrieval across scholarly indices with automated deduplication
- Structured filter extraction from natural language queries
- Profile-aware personalization

The system synthesizes complex biomedical findings into plain, patient-friendly language — a critical breakthrough for health literacy in underserved patient communities. collabiora’s initial deployment has focused on Parkinson’s disease communities, building active partnerships with patient advocacy organizations and support networks across Los Angeles.

---

## HIPAA-aligned, compliance-first platform

The Yori platform is built to HIPAA compliance standards, with anonymized patient profiles and clinician verification through ORCID, an internationally recognized academic researcher identification system.

This commitment to data privacy and regulatory compliance positions collabiora competitively among healthcare AI startups seeking institutional adoption across universities, hospitals, pharma and biotech R&D teams, and health plans. The company is actively building partnerships with leading academic medical institutions and clinical research centres.

> “People keep asking us how Yori is different from just asking an AI a question. The answer is that Yori was trained to care about the right answer, not just a fast one. That distinction matters enormously when someone’s health is on the line.” — Esther Feldman, Co-Founder, collabiora
